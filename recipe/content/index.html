Homemade Funfetti Cupcakes Recipe


Research Evaluation Docs: Ella Jang Recipe Research Evaluation

Recipe Research Description:

This recipe explores homemade Funfetti cupcakes made from simple, scratch ingredients. The cupcakes are light, fluffy, and vanilla-scented, topped with a smooth, piped frosting and finished with colorful sprinkles. Ideal for birthdays and versatile enough for any celebration.

Ingredients

Butter, Salt, Baking Powder, Baking Soda, Vanilla, Eggs, Sugar, Flour, Milk, Powdered Sugar, Sour Cream, Sprinkles, Heavy Cream

Equipment

Standard 12-cup muffin tin, Cupcake liners, Mixing bowls, Measuring cups and spoons, Stand Mixer (Recommended) OR Handheld Mixer, Spatula or Wooden Spoon

Time Estimate

  • Prep Time: 15 minutes
  • Bake Time: 18–23 minutes
  • Total Estimated Time: 60 minutes (Includes ample time for cooling, frosting, and decorating)

Dietary Restrictions / Allergens

  • Dairy: The recipe uses butter, sour cream, milk, and heavy cream.
  • Gluten: All-purpose flour is a primary ingredient.
  • Eggs: The recipe calls for two eggs.

Instructions

1. Mix Dry Ingredients: Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
2. Cream Butter and Sugar: In a mixer, beat butter and sugar until fluffy.
3. Add Eggs and Vanilla: Mix in eggs and vanilla extract.
4. Incorporate Dairy: Blend in sour cream and milk.
5. Combine Wet and Dry Ingredients: Gradually add the dry mix to the wet mixture until smooth.
6. Add Sprinkles: Gently fold in the rainbow sprinkles.
7. Fill Cupcake Liners: Fill 12 paper-lined muffin cups with batter.
8. Bake: Bake at 350°F for 18-23 minutes. Let them cool.
9. Prepare Frosting: Beat butter and salt until creamy. Mix in sugar, cream, and vanilla. Beat until fluffy, adding more cream if needed.
10. Frost and Decorate: Pipe frosting onto cooled cupcakes and add sprinkles.

Source: Preppy Kitchen

Serving and Storage

  • Serving Temperature: These cupcakes are best served at room temperature.
  • Storage: Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Allow them to come back to room temperature before serving for the best texture.

Recipe Websites

1. Preppy Kitchen offers a visually polished and user-friendly recipe layout with a strong emphasis on structure and clarity. The site makes excellent use of hierarchy through clear headings, step numbering, and high-quality imagery that supports each instruction. A floating recipe card enhances usability by keeping key information accessible while scrolling. From a UX standpoint, the inclusion of pro tips, ingredient notes, and storage advice helps users feel more confident in their baking.
2. RecipeTin Eats offers a clear and practical recipe layout that works well for both beginners and experienced bakers. The use of bold headings, step images, and well-structured content makes it easy to follow. It also does a great job of explaining the “why” behind each step, which helps build trust and confidence. However, the page layout feels a bit crowded, with all the content pushed to the right side and surrounded by ads and sidebars. This can make it harder to read and slightly overwhelming. Adding more white space or a simplified view could improve the overall user experience.
3. Cupcake Project uses a traditional blog layout with a clear recipe block and helpful additions like FAQs and user questions. The friendly tone and practical baking tips make it approachable for home bakers. However, the overall layout could be improved: the main image is quite large, which pushes content down and makes it harder to view everything at once. The steps are also not numbered, which can be confusing for beginners trying to follow along. On the positive side, embedded links throughout the post are useful, allowing users to easily explore specific techniques or tools in more detail. More consistent spacing and visual organization would improve readability, especially on smaller screens.

Non-recipe Websites

1. Dropbox ’s design is minimalist and distraction free, with a strong focus on usability. Menus are simple, typography is clean, and calls to action are clear and consistent. For recipe design, emulating this means minimizing visual clutter (ads, unrelated links), keeping formatting consistent, and making key actions prominent (ex. “print recipe,” “save to favorites).
2. Notion combines flexibility and structure: clean grids, collapsible sections, and modular content blocks. Users can expand or collapse what they need, reducing overload. Translating this to recipe UI could mean using collapsible instruction steps, tooltips or expandable sections.
3. Medium Medium excels in content readability through clean typography, generous white space, and a distraction-free interface. Articles are broken up with subheadings, callouts, and high-contrast pull quotes. For recipe content, I can incorporate generous spacing, bold headings, and minimal styling to help users stay focused while cooking.
